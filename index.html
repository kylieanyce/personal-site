About Me

Technology has always been a part of my life; from my obsession with Tamagotchis in third grade, to spending a summer 'playing' Mavis Beacon at my grandmother's, to Myspace and Instagram and Wordpress. However, the past few years made me want to focus on activism, so I enrolled at Tennessee State University in their Urban Studies program with hopes to work in the field of environmental justice. The program was intense and working in that field could be emotionally draining, so after a couple of years I needed a break and in turn, a change. I began looking at other options and computer science was suggested to me. After all, I'm great at organization, detailed, driven, and love learning! Some friends in the field suggested ways that I can tie my passion for nature and sustainability with working as a developer. I weighed my options and decided Nashville Software School would be the way to go and I haven't regretted a single minute of it!

Native Plant Rescue

Front End Capstone

Inspired by wanting to save native plants from being thrown out through landscaping and remodeling, I created a single page application that allows users to exchange plants for free. It has full CRUD functionality; and was built with React, HTML, and CSS.

Sprinkles of Joy

Front End Group Project

Sprinkles of Joy is a single page application built for a bakery! Built with JavaScript, HTML, and CSS; the user can browse items and add to their cart, view their order history, see reviews, and organize items by type.

Holiday Road Travel Guide

Front End Group Project

This travel guide is a single page application built with JavaScript, CSS, and HTML. We used four external API's to display a selction of parks, restaurants, and attractions for which the weather would then be displayed. The user could then create, save, and delete travel itineraries.
